Condition Probability

The Conditional Probability of Event A given that event B occurs (or Conditional on B occurring) is

P(A∣B)=P(A∩B)P(B)P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} P(A∣B)=P(B)P(A∩B)​

Sometimes P(A∣B)=P(A)P(A|B) = P(A)P(A∣B)=P(A) ie. P(Raining in Denmark∣Wearing Socks inMelbourne)P(Raining \ in \ Denmark | Wearing \ Socks \ in Melbourne )P(Raining in Denmark∣Wearing Socks inMelbourne), In this case one event does not affect the other as in they are independent events.

In General howver extra information changes the conditional prob of an event. ie:

P(it rains in hawthorn∣it is cloudy)P(it \ rains \ in \ hawthorn|it \ is \ cloudy) P(it rains in hawthorn∣it is cloudy)

in this case P(A∣B)>P(A)P(A|B) > P(A)P(A∣B)>P(A) (fair assumption).In General,

P(A∣B)̸=P(A)P(A|B) \neq P(A) P(A∣B)̸=P(A)

for P(A|B) = \frac{P(A\capB)}{P(B)} effectively, the sample sapce has been reduced to the outcomes in B

** Note ** Remember to distinguish between independent and mutually exclusive events. Independent events do not affect each but can both occur at the same time whereas mutually exclusive events cannot occur at the same time.

If A and B are Independent then:

P(A∣B)=P(A)P(B∣A)=P(A)P(B)P(A∩B)=P(A)P(B)\begin{aligned} P(A|B) &= P(A) \\ P(B|A) &= P(A)P(B) \\ P(A \cap B) &= P(A)P(B) \end{aligned} P(A∣B)P(B∣A)P(A∩B)​=P(A)=P(A)P(B)=P(A)P(B)​
